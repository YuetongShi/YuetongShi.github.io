I grew up in Harbin, located in the northeast of China, which is the most beautiful place in the world. In August 2021, at my age of 19, I moved to Montreal for university. I'm currently a U3 computer science student at McGill University, and I'm graduating at the end of Winter 2025 semester.

Say why computer science was selected as my major is that I have been fascinated by Rubik's Cubes since 2014, while I saw numerous experts on it, including robots. It left a deep impression on me when I saw a robot solving a 3 x 3 cube in only 1 second. Even the world record holder would still need 6.55s to solve a same cube at that time, how can it be so intelligent? However, when electronic cubes became increasingly popular in later years, things became way more magical and mysterious for me. I found it surprisingly cool that computers not only connected to the timer on my cellphone, but also tracked the cube in my hand. Since then, I decided to learn about computer science in order to discover the magic behind computers. And luckily of me, I was accepted by school of computer science at McGill.
